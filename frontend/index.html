Health Tips

How often should I visit a doctor?

For healthy adults, an annual check-up is generally recommended. However, if you have chronic conditions like diabetes, heart disease, or high blood pressure, you may need more frequent visits every 3-6 months. Always consult your healthcare provider for personalized advice based on your health status.

What should I bring to my appointment?

Bring your insurance card, a list of current medications, previous test results, and a list of questions you want to ask. If it's your first visit, bring your medical history and any relevant family health history. Don't forget to mention any allergies or adverse reactions to medications.

How can I maintain good heart health?

Maintain a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Exercise regularly for at least 30 minutes daily. Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption. Manage stress through relaxation techniques. Monitor your blood pressure and cholesterol levels regularly, and maintain a healthy weight.

What are the warning signs I shouldn't ignore?

Seek immediate medical attention for chest pain, difficulty breathing, sudden severe headache, persistent high fever, unexplained weight loss, changes in vision, severe abdominal pain, or any symptoms that worsen rapidly. Trust your instincts - if something feels seriously wrong, don't hesitate to seek help.

How can I boost my immune system naturally?

Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours nightly), eat a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables, exercise regularly, manage stress, stay hydrated, and maintain good hygiene. Consider supplements like vitamin D and zinc if recommended by your doctor. Avoid excessive alcohol and smoking.

What lifestyle changes can prevent diabetes?

Maintain a healthy weight, eat a balanced diet low in processed sugars and refined carbs, exercise regularly, limit portion sizes, and avoid sugary drinks. Include fiber-rich foods, lean proteins, and healthy fats in your diet. Regular health screenings can help detect early signs of diabetes.

How can I improve my mental health?

Practice mindfulness and meditation, maintain social connections, engage in regular physical activity, get enough sleep, eat a balanced diet, limit alcohol and avoid drugs. Don't hesitate to seek professional help when needed. Consider therapy, support groups, or counseling services.

How to Book an Ambulance

What information do I need to provide when booking an ambulance?

You need to provide your exact pickup location, destination (hospital/clinic), patient's condition, contact number, and any special medical requirements. Clear address details help our drivers reach you faster.

How quickly can an ambulance reach my location?

Our average response time is 8-15 minutes in urban areas and 15-25 minutes in rural areas. Emergency cases are prioritized and may receive faster response times.

What types of ambulances are available?

We offer basic life support (BLS) ambulances for stable patients, advanced life support (ALS) ambulances with cardiac monitors, and ICU ambulances for critical care transport.

What are the ambulance service charges?

Basic ambulance starts from ‡ß≥500 for city limits, ALS ambulance from ‡ß≥800, and ICU ambulance from ‡ß≥1200. Additional charges may apply for long distances or special medical equipment.

Can I track the ambulance location?

Yes, once your booking is confirmed, you'll receive real-time tracking updates via SMS and app notifications. You can see the ambulance's current location and estimated arrival time.

Connecting Lives Through Blood Donation

How to Request Blood

What information do I need to provide for a blood request?

You need to provide the patient's name, blood group, required units, hospital/clinic name, contact number, and urgency level. Additional medical documents may be helpful for urgent cases.

How quickly can I expect to find a donor?

Response time varies based on blood group availability and location. Common blood groups typically find matches within 2-6 hours, while rare blood groups may take 12-24 hours. Emergency cases are prioritized.

Is there any cost for requesting blood?

Our blood matching service is completely free. However, you may need to cover transportation costs for the donor and any medical screening fees required by the hospital or blood bank.

What blood groups are most needed?

O negative (universal donor) and AB positive (universal plasma donor) are always in high demand. Rh-negative blood groups are particularly rare in Bangladesh and highly needed for emergency cases.

Can I request blood for someone else?

Yes, you can request blood on behalf of a family member or friend. Please ensure you have accurate information about the patient and permission to make the request. Hospital verification may be required.
